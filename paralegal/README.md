# Paralegal Plugin for Claude

Draft legal documents, summarize cases, and write client correspondence.

Built by [The AI Career Lab](https://theaicareerlab.com/professions/paralegal) -- AI tools, guides, and weekly digests designed specifically for paralegals.

## Commands

| Command | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| `/legal-draft` | Draft contracts, demand letters, discovery responses, motions, and legal memoranda with proper formatting and standard clauses |
| `/research-memo` | Create structured legal research memos using IRAC format with issue analysis and applicable law |
| `/case-summary` | Generate case summaries, deposition digests, and chronological case timelines from provided details |
| `/client-letter` | Write professional client status updates, engagement letters, case summaries, and closing letters |

## Skills

This plugin includes skills that activate automatically when you're working on legal tasks:

- **Legal Writing** -- Contract drafting, legal memoranda, discovery responses, correspondence, jurisdiction-specific formatting, legal terminology, and privilege awareness
- **Legal Research** -- Case analysis, statutory interpretation, regulatory research, precedent identification, IRAC methodology, and citation formats

## Usage examples

```
/legal-draft
Document type: Contract. Case context: Software development agreement between
TechStart Inc. (developer) and MegaCorp LLC (client) for custom CRM platform.
Key terms: Fixed fee $150,000, 6-month delivery timeline, intellectual property
assigned to client upon final payment. Jurisdiction: State of Delaware.
```

```
/research-memo
Research question: Whether an employer can enforce a non-compete agreement
against an employee terminated without cause. Facts: Employee signed non-compete
at hiring, worked for 5 years as sales director, terminated during restructuring.
Non-compete: 18 months, 100-mile radius, same industry. Jurisdiction: Texas.
Area of law: Employment law.
```

```
/case-summary
Summary type: Deposition digest. Case details: Deposition of Dr. James Wilson,
treating physician in personal injury case Martinez v. City Transit Authority.
Dr. Wilson treated plaintiff for cervical spine injuries following bus accident.
Testified that injuries are consistent with collision mechanism described.
Estimated 12-18 months additional treatment needed. Key issues: Causation of
injuries, extent of future medical treatment, pre-existing conditions.
```

```
/client-letter
Letter purpose: Status update. Client: Maria Rodriguez.
Case details: Employment discrimination claim filed with EEOC 3 months ago.
Employer has responded to the charge. EEOC investigation is ongoing.
Key points: 1) Received employer's position statement. 2) EEOC has requested
additional documentation from both parties. 3) Mediation may be offered within
60 days. 4) Next steps and estimated timeline for resolution.
```

## Disclaimer

All content generated with this plugin is a professional draft requiring attorney review before use or distribution. This plugin does not provide legal advice. The supervising attorney is responsible for verifying all citations, ensuring jurisdiction-specific accuracy, and exercising independent professional judgment. AI-generated legal citations may not reflect actual case law and must be independently verified.
